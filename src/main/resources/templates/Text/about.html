3.The Image Viewed from the inside of Canada

Culture Canada

From now on, I want to talk about my experience after arriving in Canada. First, I want to write about the many things I have felt through my daily life. Actually, I felt and experienced many things while living here with my children and husband. The most importantly, there are people from many different cultures in Canada. For example, my daughter’s class has friends from many countries. They are friends who were originally born in Canada or from Spain, China and Brazil; therefore, I met lots of parents from many different countries. They are all very kind and helpful to me as someone who experienced Canada before me. At first, I couldn’t speak English very well, so I was very worried about talking to them. However, they gladly helped me, kindly explained and invited me. The kindness and consideration for the stranger was the first impression of me and the children about the Canadians.


Various People


Moreover, Canadians and Canadian culture are too different from South Korea. The experiences were not known before coming to Canada, so they were even more surprising. For instance, the teacher is like a friend, we call the teacher’s name. I can’t imagine it in Korea. When I first came in George Brown College, I call my teacher “teacher”, but she explained to me that it’s like she calls me “student”, so I called her name. This is a cultural difference in language and title. Second, I feel a lot of cultural differences on the subway. The telephone and the Internet are not working well on the subway, so the subway is relatively quiet; in contrast, subway in Korea were always noisy with the voices of people on the phone. Also, the seat is empty, Canadian usually ask me to sit down first.


The subway


Third, Canadian use the words sorry, excuse me, or thank you often or daily. These are the words I hear the most, and the words I speak the most here.


I am sorry


Canadians and Americans seem to be much different including their culture, characteristics, history or thought. Indeed, Canada and the United states are different countries and these have different cultures. It is as if Japan or China and south Korea is the same.

Canadian Culture

Finally, Canada I thought and Canada I experienced myself had the same and wrong. Not all Canadian regions are as cold as the Arctic, and I am doing better than expected. Of course, I still need thick clothes and sometimes I feel like flying in a strong wind. Furthermore, Canada has beautiful nature environment and it is famous for Ice Hockey, and maple syrup including many parks, tasty wine and wonderful maple. These are all true.



4. Conclusion

Culture Canada

In conclusion, the experience and images before and after coming to Canada have different things and the same things. I think the culture that should be appreciated the most in Canada's is to respect diversity and to be considerate and respectful of others. The Canadians that I experienced recognize each other, respect each other’s culture, and live together learning and feeling. They are all different at fist glance, but if you look closely, they are all the same as Canada.
