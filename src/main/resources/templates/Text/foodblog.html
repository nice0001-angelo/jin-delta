For Our future


We thought a lot about our life, cooking, our home countries and Canada while starting this food blog. For example, Korea offers free lunch to all of public school students. Surprisingly, it is not low quality meals and is also not cheap ingredients. Nutritionists manage daily diets carefully and use organic ingredients. There is even parents monitors regularly. Most children love school lunch very much and to be honest, it is often tastier than mom’s. There is always fresh fruits and vegetables on every week day and there is birthday cake and ice cream on a special day. Of course, Canadian mothers didn’t believe this so far! They said Canadian consisted of various races; therefore, a nutritionist can’t cook according to the tastes of all students. Their prediction is very interesting. How can we apply the merits of our home countries here? How can Canadians live more comfortably? The answer to this will be our task in the future. Ultimately, we want to contribute to Canada in the field of cooking and baking by making our effort and more creative ideas.


As a first step to become a good chef, now we basically have to eat hard to distinguish between good taste and aroma. Let’s continue eating over and over!



Lovely fat man eating and eating: Baek Jong Won Chef


Who is he


Baek Jong Won is known as a talented Korean chef, Baek is on the Entertainment TV program called Baek Jong-won's Alley Restaurant. It is a South Korean cooking-variety program that has been broadcast since January 5, 2018. It is a spin-off of cooking-variety program Baek Jong-won's Top 3 Chef King. It is currently hosted by Baek Jong-won and Gim Seong-ju, it aired on SBS every Friday at 23:20 (KST). It is a program that Baek go to a food alley where you are losing your vitality and do consulting, reviving..

                        

Why we choose him...


Baek Jong Won grew up in a rich family, entered a top university in south korea, and was always in a stable environment, but all he wanted was not economic stability but making taste food. For example, Baek, who graduated from the Department of Social Work (not Culinary) had a very interesting university life. Instead of studying, he traveled across the country in search of tasty restaurants, which helped him accumulate an extensive and comprehensive knowledge of food. He would often cook for his classmates and soon became a central figure in his department. Baek, enjoy eating and likes friendly, simple, but delicious rice and food more than expensive and complex luxury dishes. It is Korean homemade food. It is a home-made meal including a mother's finger touch and heart. His pursuit of Food style similar with us, Jee and Mitchell, and It is the reason we choose him.


What We will Do
