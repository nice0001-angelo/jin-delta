Our Yesterday and Today


Michelle

I am a lifestyle blogger and studying Baking Art Management in Toronto, Canada. I’ve loved to be a writer and adventure seeker in the hospitality field in general. Fueled by traveling, cooking, baking, tasting a fusion of the cuisine. After graduating from the Bachelor of International Business, I got a good job yet just felt that everything was satisfied and smooth for the short-term. but I dare to get new experience and hope to get growth better than yesterday, day by day.

Jee

I liked to write and draw when I was very young, also I was good at all kinds of sports thanks to my parents. I was very quiet and was always thinking of something myself and enjoyed imaging my future and something interesting. Also, Korean education is very systematic and sometimes forced. I studied very hard and did well in math. Koreans’ level of study and academic enthusiasm seems to be the top in the world. I don’t like this Korean style education, but ironically, this method of education is the driving power behind my lifestyle, patience, and persistence. Practically, I strongly believe that there is nothing human cannot do I graduated from university, and I worked for a public company. After I got married, my life changed dramatically. I am living so busy with my husband and children, my present life is absolutely not quiet, and my family comes before me now.

                                                                                              




Inspiration from mother

Michelle

I found a real passion in my career which is inspired by my mother and our home’s kitchen. My potential culinary capacity was built-up by the mother who taught me fundamental knowledge and transmitted valuable experiences during her cooking journey.

Jee

When it was my first time and not easy to take care of my baby, I got a lot of help from my mother. She always gave me courage, hope, and support. She is the owner-chef of a Japanese restaurant in Korea. Furthermore, she is good at all Korean cuisine. Her strengths are thorough hygiene and consistent devotion. I started to help her job and I learned and realized a lot from serving, cashiers, kitchen assistants and even buying ingredients. This was a real-world of the restaurant, not a practice. Now and forever, I think the greatest thing in the world is a mother.

For Our future


We thought a lot about our life, cooking, our home countries, and Canada while starting this food blog. For example, South Korea offers free lunch to all public school students. Surprisingly, it is not low-quality meals and is also not cheap ingredients. Nutritionists manage daily diets carefully and use organic ingredients. There are even parents monitors regularly. Most children love school lunch very much and to be honest, it is often tastier than moms. There are always fresh fruits and vegetables every weekday and there are birthday cake and ice cream on a special day. Of course, Canadian mothers didn’t believe this so far! They said Canadian consisted of various races; therefore, a nutritionist can’t cook according to the tastes of all students. Their prediction is very interesting. How can we apply the merits of our home countries here? How can Canadians live more comfortably? The answer to this will be our task in the future. Ultimately, we want to contribute to Canada in the field of cooking and baking by making our effort and more creative ideas.

As a first step to become a good chef, now we basically have to eat hard to distinguish between good taste and aroma. Let’s continue eating over and over!






Lovely fat man-eating and eating: Baek Jong Won Chef


Who is he?

Baek Jong Won is known as a talented Korean chef, Baek is on the Entertainment TV program called Baek Jong-won's Alley Restaurant. It is a South Korean cooking-variety program that has been broadcast since January 5, 2018. It is a spin-off of cooking-variety program Baek Jong-won's Top 3 Chef King. It is currently hosted by Baek Jong-won and Gim Seong-ju, it aired on SBS(Seoul Broadcasting System) every Friday at 23:20 (KST-Korea Standard Time). It is a program that Baek go to a food alley where you are losing your vitality and do consulting, reviving...

                                                              

Why we choose him?

Baek Jong Won grew up in a rich family, entered a top university in South Korea, and was always in a stable environment, but all he wanted, was not economic stability but making tasty food. For example, Baek, who graduated from the Department of Social Work (not Culinary) had a very interesting university life. Instead of studying, he traveled across the country in search of tasty restaurants, which helped him accumulate an extensive and comprehensive knowledge of food. He would often cook for his classmates and soon became a central figure in his department. Baek, enjoy eating and likes friendly, simple, but delicious rice and food more than expensive and complex luxury dishes. It is Korean homemade food. It is a home-made meal including a mother's finger touch and heart. , Jee and Michelle, and It is the reason we choose him.

What We will Do?

We will use Baek Jong-won's recipe and add creativity with fresh ingredients to create our unique dish.
