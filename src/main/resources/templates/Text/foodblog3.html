Introduction

Sujebi is a Korean traditional soup consisting of dough flakes roughly torn by hand, with various vegetables. The flavor and recipe resemble kalguksu, except that the latter is made with noodles rather than wheat flakes. It is commonly considered a dish to consume on rainy days, along with bindaetteok. The broth for sujebi is usually made with dried anchovies, shellfish, and kelp. In order to obtain a rich, umami flavor, the ingredients should be simmered for many hours. Added to this broth are soft noodles and various vegetables or kimchi, most often zucchini and potatoes. Chef Baek Jongwon uses a lot of seasonal ingredients or meat toppings, but today I will make my own traditional Sujebi inspired by Chef Baek Jongwon and my mom's recipe.  Baek and my mom's recipe.

Instructions

 Hi everyone I am Jee, from now, I will make Korean traditional hot food. It called Sujebi. Sujebi is a Korean wheat dish. The term "Su" means hand, while the "Jebi" refers to "folded" or "folding". Sujebi is today considered a typical commoner's food, but in the past, it was relatively rare and used for special occasions. The main key point of “Sujebi” is the taste of the broth. Broth made by water, onion, radish, anchovy, sea tangle, and condiments like salt is not needed. Next, the dough will be made by hand. after being made, the dough will be into the tasty broth. Sujebi served as a Soup bowl of fresh ingredients that potato, pumpkin, onion. Depending on one’s taste, sliced Seaweed can be added. I will try to cook it with my recipe that learned from my mother. Please enjoy my Food.  


1. Preparing ingredients

-Prepare cooking tools and ingredients.
-Wash the ingredients in clean water.
-Chop the carrots, Onion, potato, Pumpkin, Radish.




2. Making Broth

-Put 10 cup water in a large pot, and add onion, radish, dried anchovies, and dried kelp.
-Bring it to a boil for 10 minutes over high heat, then lower the heat to simmer for another 10 minutes.
-Use a dry net to pull out ingredients.
-Take the anchovies, kelp, onion, radish out.
-Add pumpkin, Potato, Carrots, and 1 sp garlic paste into a pot and boil for 10 minutes.




3. Making Dough

-Put the wheat flour of 300g into the bowl.
-Combine the wheat flour, 1/2 cup water, cooking oil in a large bowl.
Put the dough into a plastic bag and keep it in the refrigerator for aging.
*tip: Using a food processor is very convenient and saves time. If you use a food processor, use the dough blade and knead all the above ingredients for 1 minute until the dough sticks together and gets lumpy.




4. Making Noodle

Put the dough in the left hand, and pull and stretch it with the right hand. Get it as thin as you can. Then tear it into bite-sized pieces with the right hand. Drop it into the boiling soup. Repeat this until the dough runs out.
-Cook for a couple of minutes to let the noodles cook. The noodles will float on the surface when cooked properly.
-Add the green onion and toasted sesame oil.


4. Plating food

Transfer to a bowl and serve hot with kimchi, and take it with kimchi according to your taste.



Review


Sujebi is a Korean traditional food that for many Koreans evokes memories of their grandmothers who cut a towering figure in their household’s kitchens. Sujebi is also a very memorable dish for many Koreans who went through poor times in their younger years, especially since Korea is now the 11th largest economy in the world. I have a good memory of eating sujebi with my parents, my grandparents. Especially when the winter season comes, I remember this strongly. this hearty and wholesome comfort food will help beat the cold and warm you up when it cold outside. Enjoying sujebi with hot broth. It will be truly food for you.
