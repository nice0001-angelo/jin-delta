Introduction

Sujebi is a popular dish in South Korea made from deep and rich broth, vegetables, and hand-kneaded flour. Interestingly, Koreans express "cool" when eating hot soup. The secret lies in the deep and subtle taste of the broth. The key point of today's Sujebi is how to make broth. If you learn this broth, you can easily apply it in many Korean soup dishes such as fish cake soup, noodles soup or Jjigae(Korean stew), etc. In addition, when the hand-kneaded flour is aged in the refrigerator for about a day, its texture adds a great flavor. It will fascinate your taste like soft jelly. Chef Jong-won Baek, who prefers the spicy taste, added Kimchi and spicy pepper to the soup; It is called Kimchi Sujebi. However, today I will make a sweet, savory Sujebi, not spicy for you and my children. I remember hearing about Sujebi many times from my teacher, Anna, as her favorite Korean food; she reminded me to make this dish. Especially on rainy or cold days, when you want to melt your body and mind with something warm, I highly recommend this dish to you.

Instructions

Sujebi is a wheat dish based on broth. The term "Su" means hand, while the "Jebi" refers to "folded" or "folding".The main key point of “Sujebi” is the taste of the broth. The broth is made of water, onion, radish, anchovy, kelp, but condiments like salt or soy are not needed. Next, the dough will be made by hand. After being made, the process of aging is necessary. Aging is good for about a day, yet if you are in a hurry, you need to refrigerate it for hours. Depending on one’s taste, sliced seaweed toppings can be added to the completed Sujebi. The detailed recipe is as follows.  

1. Preparing ingredients

          

1. Prepare a large pot, 10 cup water, onion, radish, dried anchovies, dried kelp, carrot, zucchini, green onion, wheat flour, garlic paste, salt, and cooking oil.

2. Cut the zucchini, potato, and onion into 0.5-centimeter thick  pieces (half-moon shape)

3. Finely chop the green onions, and slice the carrots into 3cm long pieces.
  

2. Making broth

 

 

1. Put 10 cup water in a large pot, and add onion, radish, dried anchovies, and dried kelp.

2. Boil them for 10 minutes on high heat, then boil for another 10 minutes over low heat.

3. Use a dry net to pull out anchovies, kelp, onion, radish.

4. when the broth boils, add potatoes and onions and boil for 5 minutes.


3. Making dough

 

1. Put the wheat flour of 300g into the bowl, and combine the wheat flour, 1/2 cup of water, and 3 teaspoons cooking oil.

2. Put the dough into a plastic bag and keep it in the refrigerator for aging(1 day).

*tip: Using a food processor is very convenient and saves time. If you use a food processor, use the dough blade and knead all the above ingredients for 1 minute until the dough sticks together and gets lumpy.


4. Making noodle

 

1. Put the dough in the left hand, and pull and stretch it with the right hand. Get it as thin as you can. Then tear it into bite-sized pieces with the right hand. Drop it into the boiling soup. Repeat this until the dough runs out.

2. Cook for a couple of minutes to let the noodles cook. The noodles will float on the surface when cooked properly. Then, add the green onion, carrot, zucchini.

5. Plating food

   

Serve in a bowl and eat with moderately ripe Kimchi. Depending on your taste, you can add seaweed powder, red pepper or soy sauce.


Review


Now, the savory aroma fills my whole house. Its clear broth is enough to excite my children. First of all, I should always taste the soup from Sujebi. Wow! I can't believe it, its taste is really deep, rich and cool! This is the taste of my hometown!  It is similar to the taste of  Seoul, which I miss even more lately. Next, it is time to eat Sujebi noodles. How can this be gooey? What jelly can be better than this in the world?  Besides, the sweet taste of ripe potatoes and a combination of zucchini, carrot, and onion is really artistic! The harmony of a  spoonful of warm Sujebi and Kimchi can make you the happiest of this moment! Now, if your heart is cold and lonely, enjoy this with me!
