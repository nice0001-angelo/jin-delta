Introduction

Bibimbap is a typical Korean dish that mixes a variety of vegetables, meat or egg, and red pepper sauce together. The Bibimbap, which is so popular and famous that even celebrities including Michael Jackson love, features traditional Korean food. The basics of Korean food are the balanced composition of the ingredients and the quality that takes a long time to complete. Although there are a lot of ingredients and it takes a long time from purchasing to preparing materials, I would like to introduce to you the food representative of well-being. Especially, I want to eat this Bibimbap with you, which can save even the palate of those who have lost their appetite or dislike vegetables. Chef JongWon Baek uses a lot of seasonal ingredients or meat toppings, but today I will make my own traditional Bibimbap inspired by Chef JongWon Baek and my mom's recipe.

Instructions
 Bibimbap is a Korean rice dish and the term "Bibim" means mixing various ingredients, while the "Bap" noun refers to rice. Bibimbap is served as a bowl of warm white rice topped with Namul (sautéed and seasoned vegetables) or Kimchi (traditional fermented vegetables) and Gochujang (chili pepper paste), soy sauce, or Doenjang (a fermented soybean paste). A raw or fried egg and sliced meat (usually beef) are common additions. The hot dish is stirred together thoroughly just before eating. 

1. Preparing ingredients

First, I prepare cooking tools and ingredients. Then wash the ingredients in clean water. Then, chop the carrots, lettuce and slice the seaweed. and cut off the head of the spinach.







2. Boiling ingredients

-After the water boils, add Spinach with 1/4 tsp(tea spoon) salt, blanch it (about 2 minutes) and  rinse blanched spinach in cold water.

-Next put water and bean sprouts in a pot After adding 1/2 tsp salt, boil it with opening the lid for 2-3 minutes, and rinsing bean sprouts in cold water.







3. Mixing or Frying ingredients

-Cut blanched spinach and bean sprouts to about 3 to 4cm in length, and add 2 tsp sesame oil and 1/4 salt and  mix them each.

-Sprinkle a little salt of carrots and fry in a frying pan for about 1 minute as quickly as possible.

-Add a handful of salt to the bracken water boiled in the mart and fry for 2-3 minutes on high heat.

-put the 1 tsp Cooking oil in the frying pan, and fry the eggs until it is half cooked.

      -  Gochujang (chili pepper paste) sauce: 

          Mix  Gochujang 2sp, sesame oil 2 tsp, cooking wine 1tsp, vinegar 1/2 tsp, and sesame 1sp(spoon) 

      -  soy sauce: Mix SOY 2sp, sesame oil 2 tsp, and sesame 1sp(spoon) 

     

4. Plating food

Place the rice in the bottom of the plate and the prepared ingredients on top of it.

And take it with chili pepper paste or soy sauce according to your taste.




Review


The savory aroma of sesame oil and sesame salt stimulates appetite and the visual of various colors already captivates me from the visual taste. Once you take a bite, a fantastic combination of colorful vegetables, half-boiled eggs, and Korean-style sauces presents a spicy, sweet, crispy and soft taste. Korean Gochujang sauce has several levels of spiciness, but it will taste better if regular Gochujang is added to suit your taste. My 9-year-old daughter mixes half soy sauce and half red pepper sauce and enjoys it fantastic! In particular, this dish may change its name and taste depending on the topping such as meat bibimbap and squid bibimbap. This goes well with wine and a luxurious atmosphere, so I strongly recommend it to you. Fantastic!
